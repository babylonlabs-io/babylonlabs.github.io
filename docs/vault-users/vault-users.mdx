# Trustless Bitcoin Vaults

Trustless Bitcoin Vaults (TBV) let you use your Bitcoin as collateral in DeFi
**without bridges, wrapping, or trusted custodians**. Your BTC stays on the
Bitcoin blockchain in a self-custodial vault while you borrow stablecoins and
other assets on Ethereum.

## What You Can Do

With TBV, the typical user flow looks like this:

1. **Deposit BTC** -- Send your Bitcoin to a vault address on the Bitcoin network.
2. **Create a vault** -- Your vault is registered on Ethereum, making your BTC
   available as collateral.
3. **Borrow stablecoins** -- Use your vault as collateral on Aave v4 to borrow
   assets like USDC.
4. **Repay and redeem** -- When you are ready, repay your loan and unlock your
   Bitcoin.

You can interact with TBV through the **web portal** or the **CLI** -- both
provide the same functionality.

## How It Works

TBV is built on three layers that work together to keep your Bitcoin secure
while making it usable in DeFi:

### Bitcoin Vault Layer

Your Bitcoin is locked in a Taproot script address on the Bitcoin network. This
script encodes all possible outcomes -- repayment, liquidation, and fraud
challenge -- as pre-signed transactions. No single party can move your BTC
without satisfying the conditions agreed upon during vault creation.

### Ethereum Coordination Layer

The **BTCVaultRegistry** contract on Ethereum tracks vault state and coordinates
the lifecycle of each vault. During creation, it records the vault request,
participant acknowledgments, and the Bitcoin inclusion proof that activates the
vault. During redemption, it records the Ethereum-side state changes that allow
the Bitcoin release flow to begin.

### Proof Generation Layer

When Bitcoin needs to verify that something happened on Ethereum (such as a vault
redemption), the protocol generates cryptographic proofs using **BitVM3** and
**Winternitz One-Time Signatures (WOTS)**. These proofs allow trustless
verification directly on Bitcoin -- no oracle or committee decides whether your
BTC can be released.

## Key Properties

- **No bridges.** Your Bitcoin never leaves the Bitcoin blockchain. There are no
  wrapped tokens, no custodial bridges, and no validator sets controlling your
  funds.
- **Self-custodial.** You create the vault, you sign the transactions, and you
  retain the ability to redeem your Bitcoin at any time after repaying any
  outstanding debt.
- **Trustless verification.** Redemption proofs are verified on Bitcoin using
  BitVM3 garbled circuits with WOTS signatures -- no trusted intermediary is
  involved.
- **~1 hour activation.** From deposit request to active vault takes
  approximately one hour, including Bitcoin confirmation time.
- **Composable.** Active vaults can be used as collateral across DeFi
  applications, starting with Aave v4 integration.
