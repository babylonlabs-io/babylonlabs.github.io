# Protocol Actors

The Trustless Bitcoin Vault protocol relies on several types of participants
working together. No single party controls your Bitcoin -- security comes from
the interaction between independent actors with different roles and incentives.

## Vault Providers

Vault providers are the infrastructure operators you interact with most
directly. When you create a vault, you select a vault provider to coordinate
the process.

**What they do for you:**

- **Coordinate vault creation.** When you submit a vault request, the vault
  provider's systems automatically handle the complex setup -- constructing
  transaction graphs, exchanging signatures with vault keepers, and collecting
  acknowledgments.
- **Generate proofs for redemption.** When you redeem your vault, the vault
  provider generates the zero-knowledge proof that allows Bitcoin to verify the
  Ethereum-side events. This is computationally intensive work requiring
  specialized GPU infrastructure.
- **Manage Bitcoin claims.** The vault provider broadcasts the claim and payout
  transactions on Bitcoin during the redemption process, delivering your BTC
  back to you.
- **Submit inclusion proofs.** After you broadcast your Bitcoin during vault
  creation, the vault provider monitors for confirmations and submits the
  proof that activates your vault.

Vault providers are registered on-chain and must meet infrastructure and
economic requirements to participate.

## Vault Keepers

Vault keepers serve as the economic backstop for vault security. They are
independent operators who co-sign transaction graphs during vault creation and
can step in during liquidation scenarios.

**What they do:**

- **Co-sign transactions.** During vault creation, vault keepers create their
  own set of pre-signed Bitcoin transactions and cross-sign with the vault
  provider. This means multiple independent parties hold the transaction data
  needed for every vault outcome.
- **Provide liquidation liquidity.** In liquidation scenarios, vault keepers can
  act as Liquidation Liquidity Providers (LLPs), acquiring liquidated vaults and
  handling the Bitcoin claim process. This ensures liquidated vaults are settled
  promptly.
- **Participate in challenges.** As holders of pre-signed transactions and WOTS
  key material, vault keepers can challenge fraudulent claims if they detect
  them.

Vault keepers are registered per application through the BTCVaultRegistry
contract and are versioned -- changes require creating a new version, so
existing vaults are never affected by keeper changes.

## Universal Challengers

Universal challengers are protocol-level fraud monitors. Their job is to watch
for invalid proof submissions during vault redemption and challenge them.

**What they do:**

- **Monitor all claims.** Universal challengers watch for claim transactions on
  Bitcoin across all vaults and verify that the associated proofs are valid.
- **Challenge fraud.** If an invalid proof is detected, a universal challenger
  broadcasts a challenge transaction on Bitcoin, triggering the dispute
  resolution process using BitVM3 garbled circuits and WOTS signatures.
- **Earn rewards.** Challengers who successfully prove fraud claim the
  fraudulent claimer's bond as a reward.

Universal challengers' public keys are committed into every vault's Taproot
script, giving them the cryptographic ability to challenge any claim. They
operate at the protocol level, not per-application, providing security across
all vaults.

**Why they matter.** Even if a vault provider and all vault keepers were to
collude, universal challengers can prevent invalid Bitcoin claims by initiating
challenges. They are the protocol's last line of defense.

## Security Council

The security council is a group of entities that serves as an emergency
backstop. Their keys are committed into the protocol's parameters.

**What they do:**

- **Emergency intervention only.** The security council can intervene if the
  normal challenge mechanism fails or if a critical protocol issue requires
  immediate action.
- **Quorum-based decisions.** Any action requires multiple council member
  signatures, preventing any single member from acting alone.

The security council is **not** involved in normal protocol operation. The
protocol is designed to function entirely without their intervention under
standard conditions.

## Why Multiple Actors Means Trustless

The reason TBV can be trustless is that no single party has unilateral control
over your Bitcoin:

- Your vault provider coordinates the process, but vault keepers independently
  co-sign all transactions.
- If your vault provider disappears, you can self-claim using your WOTS key
  material.
- If anyone submits a fraudulent proof, universal challengers will catch it.
- If all else fails, the security council provides a last-resort backstop.

This layered approach means you do not need to trust any individual participant
-- security comes from the system's design.

## Actor Summary

| Actor | Role | Scope | Key Responsibility |
|-------|------|-------|--------------------|
| Vault Provider | Infrastructure operator | Per-application | Vault lifecycle management, proof generation |
| Vault Keeper | Security backstop | Per-application (versioned) | Transaction co-signing, liquidation settlement |
| Universal Challenger | Fraud monitor | Protocol-wide (versioned) | Detecting and challenging invalid proofs |
| Security Council | Emergency backstop | Protocol-wide | Emergency intervention only |
