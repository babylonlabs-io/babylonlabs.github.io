# Fees

This page covers the fees you encounter when using Trustless Bitcoin Vaults.
For Aave-specific lending fees (interest rates, liquidation bonuses), see the
Aave v4 documentation.

{/* Diagram placeholder */}

## What You Pay

### Peg-In Fee

When you submit a vault creation request, a small fee in **ETH** is charged.
This fee prevents spam and is paid as part of your vault creation transaction
on Ethereum. The fee amount is set as a protocol parameter.

### Bitcoin Transaction Fees

Standard Bitcoin miner fees apply when you broadcast your peg-in transaction.
A typical peg-in transaction is approximately 154 vbytes, so the fee depends
on current Bitcoin network fee rates.

### Ethereum Gas

All Ethereum transactions incur standard gas costs. Here are the approximate
gas costs for common operations:

| Operation | Approximate Gas |
|-----------|----------------|
| Submit vault creation request | ~278,000 |
| Acknowledgment submission (per participant) | ~90,000-204,000 |
| Inclusion proof and activation | ~600,000 |
| Add collateral to lending position | Varies by vault count |
| Borrow from position | ~200,000 |
| Repay debt | ~210,000 |
| Withdraw collateral | ~200,000 |
| Liquidation | ~500,000+ |

Gas costs vary with network congestion and the number of vaults involved.

## What Happens During Redemption

During normal redemption, Bitcoin transaction costs for the claim and payout
are paid by the **vault provider** (or vault keeper in liquidation scenarios),
not by you. The total is approximately 2,000-3,000 satoshis at typical fee
rates.

If a challenge occurs (a rare event), additional Bitcoin transactions are
required. Challenge costs are higher but are borne by the participants involved
in the dispute, not by depositors.

## Liquidation Fees

If your position is liquidated, the following fees apply:

### Liquidation Fee

When a liquidated vault is settled, a fee in basis points is deducted from the
proceeds. For example, at 100 bps (1%), a vault holding 1 BTC yields 0.99 BTC
worth of value to the liquidator.

### Fairness Payment

Even during liquidation, you receive a **fairness payment** -- a portion of
value returned to you as the original depositor. This is denominated in the
fairness payment token (typically USDC).

### Core Spoke Liquidation Fee

When a lending position on the Aave v4 Core Spoke is liquidated, the protocol
charges a fee calculated as a percentage of the liquidation bonus. This is paid
by the liquidator as part of the liquidation execution.

## BTCVaultSwap Fees

When liquidated vaults are traded between liquidators and vault keepers
(Liquidation Liquidity Providers), the settlement process involves:

- **Liquidator fee** -- Deducted from proceeds when a liquidator escrows a
  vault for sale.
- **LLP discount** -- The purchasing vault keeper receives a discount on the
  purchase price.
- **Protocol fee** -- The difference between the liquidator fee and the LLP
  discount goes to the protocol.

While a vault is in escrow, interest accrues on the drawn amount. If the
accumulated interest makes a vault unprofitable to settle, an emergency
mechanism returns the vault to the protocol to prevent indefinite debt buildup.

## Fee Flow Summary

```
You pay:
  +-- Peg-in fee (ETH) ------------------> Protocol fee collector
  +-- Bitcoin tx fee (BTC) ---------------> Bitcoin miners
  +-- Ethereum gas (ETH) -----------------> Ethereum validators

Vault provider pays:
  +-- Registration fee (ETH, one-time) ---> Protocol fee collector
  +-- Challenge bonds (BTC) --------------> Locked on Bitcoin
  +-- Claim/payout tx fees (BTC) ---------> Bitcoin miners
  +-- Ethereum gas for proofs (ETH) ------> Ethereum validators

During liquidation:
  +-- Liquidation fee --------------------> Protocol fee recipient
  +-- Fairness payment -------------------> You (the depositor)
  +-- Settlement fees --------------------> Protocol fee recipient
```
