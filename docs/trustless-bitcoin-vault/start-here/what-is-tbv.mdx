# What is TBV?

## The problem: only ~1% of Bitcoin is used in DeFi

Bitcoin, the largest asset in crypto, sits mostly idle in DeFi. There are several reasons:

**Infrastructure trade-offs:** every existing approach to using Bitcoin in DeFi forces the holder to give up custody, accept a bridging step, wrap the asset, or trust intermediaries. All four are precisely what Bitcoiners reject.

**Availability and accessibility** of Bitcoin were, and in some ways still are, limited. For years there was no straightforward way to move Bitcoin into and out of DeFi without giving up custody; the market was immature.

## What TBV is

**Trustless Bitcoin Vault (TBV)** is Babylon's protocol that lets Bitcoin holders use BTC as collateral in decentralized finance (DeFi) without giving up custody, bridging, wrapping, or trusting an intermediary.

Bringing Bitcoin into DeFi through a custodian or cross-chain bridge necessitates the Bitcoin holder's trust in that entity. In contrast, with TBV, the Bitcoin holder (depositor) places their trust solely in the protocol's cryptography, the Bitcoin and Ethereum networks, and the applications where they deposit their collateral, eliminating the need for a third party to hold the BTC.

The depositor's BTC stays on the Bitcoin Network, held in a Taproot script that the depositor co-signs at creation. An Ethereum-side protocol contract tracks each BTC vault, a single Bitcoin output (one UTXO) that holds the depositor's locked BTC, and lets an integrated DeFi application use it as collateral.

Cross-chain state transitions are enforced through cryptography, not by a trusted intermediary:

* **Collateral activation** atomically binds the Ethereum-side activation of Bitcoin collateral to the lockup of BTC in the co-signed Taproot script that holds the vault's BTC asset.
* **Collateral redemption** uses a BABE-based challenge procedure that lets Bitcoin verify proofs of Ethereum redemption events using only existing Bitcoin Script primitives. No Bitcoin fork is required.

## What a vault is, and what it is not

In DeFi the word "vault" usually means a capital pool where many users deposit into one shared contract. A Trustless Bitcoin Vault returns to the original meaning of "vault", closer to the secure compartment in a bank than to a DeFi capital pool. Each BTC vault is a **segregated**, depositor-owned Bitcoin output. Three properties define it:

* **The BTC is locked in a Bitcoin script that the depositor co-signed.** Every legitimate spending path is signed at vault creation by the depositor and the protocol participants. After that, no party can fabricate a new spend.
* **BTC in different vaults is segregated.** A vault is one UTXO, one set of pre-signed exit paths. Vaults are not pooled.
* **No one can rehypothecate the BTC.** The protocol contracts cannot move the BTC out of the script, lend it elsewhere, or repurpose it. The collateral does only what the script allows.

## How the trust model differs structurally

Existing approaches to using Bitcoin in DeFi rely on either a wrapped representation held by a centralized custodian or a cross-chain bridge that transfers value between networks. Both require the depositor to extend trust beyond the chains themselves: to a custodian, to a bridge operator's security, or to a federation of signers.

TBV's structure is different. Trust moves from custody to computation. The depositor's BTC stays in a Bitcoin output whose every legitimate spend is encoded in script and pre-signed at vault creation. Release of BTC on Bitcoin is gated by a cryptographic proof of the matching Ethereum event, verified inside Bitcoin Script. The depositor's own keys participate at every release path the depositor controls, so the depositor can always redeem their BTC trustlessly, without needing any other party to cooperate.

## TBV integration with Aave v4

TBV is the underlying collateral protocol. Aave v4 lending on Ethereum is the first application integration through which a depositor can use TBV to deposit native-BTC as collateral.

![TBV flow: BTC locked on Bitcoin, ZK proof verification, vaultBTC accounting on Ethereum, Aave v4 borrowing, and repayment back to Bitcoin](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/start-here/what-is-tbv/TBV1-flow.png)

Each BTC vault is created for one specific application at vault creation. The protocol is designed for multiple applications, but a vault is bound to its application from creation and cannot be moved between applications. Future application integrations would each integrate through their own adapter and would receive their own vaults.

## What can be tested today

On the open public Testnet, a depositor with an active vault can **test** borrowing stablecoins (USDC, USDT) or WBTC against it. The collateral is signet BTC, the borrowed assets are mock tokens on Sepolia, and both carry no monetary value. This is a testing environment in trustless Bitcoin DeFi end to end. After repayment, the vault is withdrawn and the signet BTC is released back to the depositor's Bitcoin address.

Multiple vaults can combine into a single borrowing position. The protocol seizes a prefix of the ordered vault list, sized to cover the target seizure. The mechanism is explained on [Liquidation risk](../use-for-lending/liquidation-risk.mdx).
