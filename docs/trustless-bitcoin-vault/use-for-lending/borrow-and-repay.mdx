# Borrow & repay

This page describes borrow and repay flows on the **Aave v4 integration** — the first DeFi application built on TBV. The TBV protocol layer below it (vault creation, redemption, security) is application-agnostic and the same across any future integration; what differs application-to-application is the borrowing mechanism, the interest model, and the liquidation rules. See [What is TBV?](../start-here/what-is-tbv) → "Two layers" for the overview.

This page covers borrowing USDC, USDT, or WBTC against an active vault, how interest accrues, and how to repay. The vault creation step that comes before is on [Create a vault](./create-a-vault). The redemption step that comes after is on [Withdraw & redeem](./withdraw-and-redeem).

An Active vault becomes usable collateral the moment you supply it to the lending market. From there, the day-to-day life of a position is the standard DeFi borrow-and-repay loop, with vault-specific behaviour around collateral structure and liquidation. This page is the operating guide for that loop.

:::info Testnet position cap
Each address can hold at most **0.4 BTC** of collateral across all positions in the Aave application. Each position can also hold at most 10 vaults. See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters) for the full list.
:::

## Before you start

You need:

* At least one Active vault. If you don't have one, start at [Create a vault](./create-a-vault).
* A small balance of testnet ETH for gas on each transaction.
* Familiarity with basic DeFi borrowing concepts: collateral, health factor, liquidation. See [Glossary](../reference/glossary) for short definitions.

## Supply your vault as collateral

From the **Collateral** card, select an Active vault in **Available** status and click **Add as collateral**. Confirm the Ethereum transaction.

What happens behind the scenes:

* vaultBTC is minted equal to the vault's BTC amount, at 8-decimal precision (1 vaultBTC = 1 BTC = 100,000,000 satoshis).
* The vaultBTC is supplied to the Babylon Core Spoke, the dedicated lending market that accepts vaultBTC as collateral.
* The vault's status moves from **Available** to **InUse**.
* The position's collateral value and health factor update.

You can add multiple vaults in a single transaction. All must be in Available status and belong to your wallet, and the position's total must remain under the cap (0.4 BTC on Testnet).

vaultBTC never leaves the protocol contracts. It is not transferable to arbitrary addresses, has no secondary market, and exists purely as the internal accounting token for vault-backed collateral. For the full architecture, see [Aave v4 integration](../technical-details/aave-v4-integration).

## Borrow stablecoins or WBTC

Borrowable assets on the current Testnet release:

* **USDC**
* **USDT**
* **WBTC**

Each asset has its own interest rate, supply cap, and borrow cap configured at the Aave v4 Hub level. For full numerical values and the rate-model parameters, see [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters).

From the **Loans** card, click **Borrow**:

1. Select the asset.
2. Enter the amount.
3. Confirm the Ethereum transaction.

The borrowed asset arrives in your Ethereum wallet, or in any address you specify as the receiver.

The borrow is rejected if:

* The resulting health factor would drop below 1.0.
* The amount exceeds the Hub's draw cap for that asset (10,000,000 units per asset on Testnet).
* The reserve is paused or frozen.

You can borrow from multiple asset reserves at once (up to 4 reserves combined, per `maxUserReservesLimit`). For example, borrow USDC and USDT in parallel against the same vaultBTC collateral. Each debt is tracked independently, but the health factor reflects the aggregate of all debts.

You can also return to **Borrow** repeatedly and borrow more as long as the resulting health factor stays above 1.0.

## How interest works

Interest on borrowed assets accrues continuously, not at scheduled intervals.

**Rate set by utilization.** Each asset's interest rate is a function of utilization: the ratio of total borrowed to total supplied for that asset on the Aave v4 Hub. Below the optimal utilization pivot, the rate climbs linearly at one slope; above the pivot, it climbs much faster.

Concrete Testnet anchors:

* **USDC and USDT:** optimal utilization 92%, base 0%, slope below optimal 4%, slope above 20%. At 50% utilization, borrow rate ≈ 2.2%. At 92% (pivot), rate = 4%. At 100%, rate = 24%.
* **WBTC:** optimal utilization 80%, base 0.25%, slope below optimal 4%, slope above 60% (reflecting WBTC scarcity premium). At the 80% pivot, rate = 4.25%. At 100%, rate ≈ 64%.

**Continuous accrual.** Interest is applied on every Ethereum block. There is no "interest cycle" or "billing period". The exact amount owed at any moment is the principal borrowed plus the interest accumulated up to that block.

**Protocol takes 10% of accrued interest.** On top of the Aave rate, the protocol collects 10% of accrued interest as a fee (`InterestFeeBps` = 1000 BPS for USDC and USDT; `liquidityFee` 2000 BPS for WBTC at the Hub level). The fee comes out of the interest portion — not added on top of the displayed rate — and is collected first on each repayment, before principal reduction. The Aave displayed rate is your effective interest cost; the protocol's slice comes out of what would otherwise go to lenders.

**Debt growth reduces the health factor.** As debt grows from accrued interest, the health factor drops. A position that was safe at borrow time can drift toward liquidation over a long enough period without repayment, even if BTC price is stable.

When BTC price rises, your collateral value rises, the health factor improves, and you can either borrow more or simply enjoy a larger safety margin.

## Health factor

The health factor is a single number summarizing how safe the position is. The formula is:

```
Health Factor = (Total Collateral Value × Collateral Factor) / Total Debt Value
```

The collateral factor for vaultBTC on Testnet is 78%. Since vaultBTC is the only collateral on the Babylon Core Spoke, the position's collateral factor is constant (positions on other spokes with heterogeneous collateral would use a weighted average).

A higher number is safer. A position becomes liquidatable when the health factor drops below 1.0.

| Health factor | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Above 1.5 | Comfortable margin above liquidation. |
| 1.2 to 1.5 | Approaching risk. Consider repaying some debt or adding collateral. |
| 1.0 to 1.2 | High risk. Repay debt or add collateral immediately. |
| Below 1.0 | Liquidatable. The position can be liquidated at any moment. |

The health factor changes with three inputs:

* **BTC price** moves the collateral value (rising BTC raises the health factor; falling BTC lowers it).
* **Accrued interest** moves the debt value upward over time.
* **Your actions:** borrowing more lowers the health factor; repaying or adding collateral raises it.

For the liquidation mechanism in detail, including what happens when the health factor reaches 1.0 and how the fairness payment returns any over-seized value, see [Liquidation risk](./liquidation-risk).

## Repay your debt

To pay down debt, open the **Loans** card and click **Repay**.

Choose:

* **Repay partial** pays down a portion of one asset's debt. The position stays open with reduced debt and an improved health factor.
* **Repay full** closes out one asset's debt entirely. If you have debt across multiple assets, repay each one separately. There is no aggregate "repay everything" call.

For each repay, the portal handles the ERC-20 approval and the repayment transaction.

When fully repaying, approve a slightly larger amount than the displayed debt. Interest accrues between the moment you query the debt and the moment the transaction confirms; a small buffer prevents the transaction from failing on a fractional shortfall. Any excess stays in your wallet.

On each repayment, the protocol's 10% interest fee is collected first from the accrued interest portion; remaining repayment then reduces principal. Partial repayments smaller than the accrued-interest fee may not reduce principal at all.

The protocol allows third-party repayment: anyone can repay debt on your position's behalf. This is the mechanism liquidators use to repay debt during seizure, and it is supported as a general feature. The borrower's debt decreases regardless of who paid.

After all debt is repaid across all asset reserves, the position is eligible to withdraw collateral. See [Withdraw & redeem](./withdraw-and-redeem).

## Add more collateral

To grow a position over time, add more vaults.

From the **Collateral** card, select one or more Active vaults in Available status and click **Add as collateral**. Confirm the Ethereum transaction.

Adding collateral:

* Mints additional vaultBTC and supplies it to the lending market.
* Increases the position's collateral value.
* Raises the health factor.

Adding collateral is one of three actions that raise the health factor: add collateral, repay debt, or wait for BTC price to rise.

Position cap reminder: on Testnet, total BTC across all vaults in one position cannot exceed 0.4 BTC, and you can have at most 10 vaults per position. Per-address cap across all your positions is also 0.4 BTC.

If the position now contains multiple vaults, the order of the list determines which vault is seized first during a liquidation. The vault at the front of the list goes first. See [Create a vault](./create-a-vault) for the two-vault distribution recommendation and [Liquidation risk](./liquidation-risk) for why ordering matters.

## Monitor your position

The portal surfaces the position's state on the **Loans** card:

* **Health factor**, current.
* **Total collateral value**, the sum of vault BTC amounts priced at the current BTC/USD oracle value.
* **Outstanding debt**, per asset and aggregate.
* **Borrow rate**, per asset, the live rate driven by utilization.

For programmatic access, the Babylon Core Spoke and the AaveAdapterLens contract expose read-only queries returning the same data. See [Aave v4 integration](../technical-details/aave-v4-integration) for the contract surfaces.
