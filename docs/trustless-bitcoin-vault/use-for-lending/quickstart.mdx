# Quickstart

The test app lets you use Bitcoin non-custodially as collateral to borrow stablecoins (USDC, USDT) or WBTC, then recover the BTC when the loan is repaid. It is built on the Trustless Bitcoin Vault (TBV) protocol, which locks BTC on the Bitcoin Network and makes it usable as collateral on Ethereum without bridging or wrapping. The app drives a full peg-in, borrow, repay, and peg-out cycle end to end.

:::info Public testnet limits
Each address and each position is capped on the Aave application. See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters.mdx) for the live numeric limits.
:::

## Before you start

You need:

* A Unisat Bitcoin wallet that supports signet.
* An Ethereum wallet on Sepolia.
* Signet BTC to deposit. Get it from the faucet listed in [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx).
* A small amount of Sepolia ETH for gas. Same faucet.

## Step 1: Open the test app

Navigate to the test app at the URL listed in [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx).

![Test app landing page](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/image-20260527-084545.png)

## Step 2: Connect wallets

Click the **Connect** button at the top of the app or on any card.

Important: the Bitcoin wallet must be set to **Taproot (P2TR)** address type before connecting. If a different address type was set initially, switch in the wallet and re-fund from the signet faucet (different address types do not share balance).

The wallet picker then opens. Select a Bitcoin wallet (Taproot address type) and an Ethereum wallet to sign the on-chain calls.

![Wallet picker](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/image-20260527-084735.png)

## Step 3: Create a vault (peg-in)

A peg-in moves BTC into a vault on Bitcoin and registers it on Ethereum. The BTC stays on Bitcoin throughout, locked in a Taproot output. Nothing is bridged or wrapped.

From the **Collateral** section, click **Deposit**. Then:

* Enter the amount in BTC (within the public-testnet caps; see [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters.mdx)).
* Select a Vault Provider from the dropdown.
* Confirm the Bitcoin and Ethereum transactions.

![Peg-in deposit](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/image-20260527-091245.png)

![Confirm Bitcoin and Ethereum transactions](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/image-20260527-091308.png)

The app then runs the off-chain setup automatically: authentication, signature collection, acknowledgments.

The app recommends splitting the deposit into two vaults: a sacrificial vault sized to cover the protocol's expected seizure amount (placed first in liquidation order), and a protected vault holding the rest (placed second). At current public-testnet parameters the sacrificial vault is the larger of the two.

![Split deposit into two vaults](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-029-20260528-105216.png)

## Step 4: Borrow stablecoins (Aave v4)

The vault is already supplied as collateral on the integrated Aave v4 market (automatic on activation).

Open the **Loans** section and click **Borrow**:

* Select an asset: USDC, USDT, or WBTC.
* Enter the amount and sign the borrow transaction.
* Wait for the portal to refresh the position preview and health factor.
* Confirm the Ethereum transaction.

![Borrow — enter amount](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-093-20260601-043515.png)

![Borrow — sign transaction](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-097-20260601-043549.png)

![Borrow — position preview](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-099-20260601-043622.png)

![Borrow — confirmed](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-100-20260601-043705.png)

The borrowed asset arrives in the connected Ethereum wallet. The app now shows the health factor, a single number summarizing how close the position is to liquidation. A higher number is safer.

## Step 5: Repay the debt (Aave v4)

From the **Loans** section, click **Repay**:

* **Repay full:** closes the entire borrow and unlocks redemption.
* **Repay partial:** pays down a portion; the position stays open with reduced debt.

:::info Two wallet prompts
Repaying usually shows an ERC-20 spending cap approval first, then the repayment transaction. Approve a small buffer because interest can accrue while the transaction is pending; any excess stays in the wallet.
:::

![Repay — form](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-101-20260601-043734.png)

![Repay — spending cap approval](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-102-20260601-043800.png)

![Repay — confirmed](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/confluence/use-for-lending/quickstart/screenshot-105-20260601-043835.png)

Interest accrues continuously until repayment, so the exact amount due is slightly higher than the principal borrowed. After full repayment, the vault is eligible for redemption.

## Step 6: Withdraw and redeem the BTC (peg-out)

Redeeming releases the underlying BTC from the vault back to the Bitcoin wallet.

From the **Collateral** section, select an **in use** vault and click **Withdraw**. Review the withdrawal amount, network fee rate, VP commission, and the nominated Bitcoin address, then confirm the Ethereum transaction.

The redemption then runs in three protocol-side phases:

1. **Claim.** The Vault Provider generates a zero-knowledge proof in the background showing the debt is repaid, then submits a redemption claim on Bitcoin. The app shows progress.
2. **Challenge period.** A window of approximately **3 days** opens during which a Vault Keeper or Universal Challenger may dispute the claim. If no valid challenge appears, the claim finalizes.
3. **Payout.** The BTC arrives at the address recorded in the vault.

The 3-day window is inherent to the proof-verification mechanism.

## If something stalls

Common scenarios and where to look:

* **Pre-PegIn still pending after a few hours:** check the Bitcoin block explorer (see [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx)). If the transaction is propagated but not confirmed, wait; signet block times can be irregular.
* **Vault stuck in Pending past ~24 hours:** the off-chain setup likely failed. The vault will transition to Expired and the peg-in fee will be refunded. See the refund path on [Create a vault](./create-a-vault.mdx) for BTC recovery.
* **Wallet won't connect or transaction rejected:** verify the wallet is on the correct network (see [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx) for chain IDs).
* **Vault Provider not responding during redemption:** the depositor self-claim path applies. See [Withdraw & redeem](./withdraw-and-redeem.mdx).

For anything else, ask in the public-testnet support channel listed on [Community & support](../reference/community-and-support.mdx).

## You've completed a full cycle

Wallets connected, signet BTC locked, internal accounting supplied, stablecoins borrowed, repaid, and BTC recovered.
