# Quickstart

This walkthrough goes through the **Aave v4 integration** — the first DeFi application built on TBV.

This page is the full happy path end to end: connect a wallet, deposit signet BTC, borrow stablecoins, repay, redeem. About 5 minutes of clicking, plus a ~2-hour wait for Bitcoin confirmations during peg-in and a ~3-day challenge window during redemption. Each step links to the matching deep page for full detail.

A Trustless Bitcoin Vault (TBV) locks BTC on the Bitcoin network and makes it usable as collateral on Ethereum without bridges or wrapping. The test app for Aave integration drives a full peg-in, borrow, repay, and peg-out cycle end to end.

The public testnet runs on signet, a Bitcoin test network. The BTC and stablecoins involved have no monetary value. Some flows are still under active development; report issues to the team via the channels listed in [Community & support](../reference/community-and-support.mdx).

:::info Public testnet limits
Each address and each position is capped on the Aave application. See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters.mdx) for the live numeric limits.
:::

## Before you start

You need:

* A Bitcoin wallet that supports signet.
* An Ethereum wallet on Sepolia.
* Signet BTC to deposit. Get it from the faucet listed in [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx).
* A small amount of Sepolia ETH for gas. The same page lists the faucet.

For the full list of supported wallets, network and RPC details, and faucet URLs, see [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx).

## Step 1: Open the test app

Navigate to the test app at the URL listed in [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx).

![App landing page in the pre-wallet state](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-01-landing.png)

## Step 2: Connect wallets

Click the **Connect** button at the top of the app or on any card.

Important: the Bitcoin wallet must be set to **Taproot (P2TR)** address type before connecting. If a different address type was set initially, switch in the wallet and re-fund from the signet faucet, because different address types do not share balance.

The wallet picker then opens. Select a Bitcoin wallet using a Taproot address type and an Ethereum wallet to sign the on-chain calls.

![Wallet picker](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-02-wallet-picker.png)

The Collateral and Loans sections are empty until a vault exists.

## Step 3: Create a vault (peg-in)

A peg-in moves BTC into a vault on Bitcoin and registers it on Ethereum. The BTC stays on Bitcoin throughout, locked in a Taproot output. Nothing is bridged or wrapped.

From the **Collateral** section, click **Deposit**. Then:

**1. Enter the amount in BTC** within the public-testnet caps. See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters.mdx).

![Peg-in deposit amount entry](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-03-deposit-amount.png)

**2. Select a vault provider from the dropdown.**

![Vault provider dropdown](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-04-provider-dropdown.png)

**3. Confirm the Bitcoin and Ethereum transactions.**

![Confirm Bitcoin + Ethereum transactions](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-05-confirm-txs.png)

The app then runs the off-chain setup automatically: authentication, signature collection, acknowledgments. The full walkthrough is on [Create a vault](./create-a-vault.mdx).

The app recommends splitting your deposit into two vaults: a sacrificial vault sized to cover the protocol's expected seizure amount (placed first in liquidation order), and a protected vault holding the rest (placed second). At current public-testnet parameters, the sacrificial vault is the larger of the two. For the order-change UI, see [Reorder BTC vaults](./create-a-vault.mdx#reorder-vaults-later).

When the vault reaches **Verified** status, the app prompts you to **Activate**. Click it. The vault transitions to **Active**, and the protocol automatically supplies it to the lending position.

![Active vault in the Collateral section](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-06-activate-prompt.png)

## Step 4: Borrow stablecoins (Aave v4 application layer)

Your vault is already supplied as collateral on the integrated Aave v4 market — minted and supplied automatically when you activated. From this step on, you are interacting with the Aave v4 application layer; the underlying vault on Bitcoin sits unchanged.

Open the **Loans** section and click **Borrow**:

**1. Select an asset: USDC, USDT, or WBTC.**

![Borrow modal — select asset](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-07-borrow-select-asset.png)

**2. Enter the amount and sign the borrow transaction.**

![Borrow modal — enter amount](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-08-borrow-enter-amount.png)

**3. Confirm the Ethereum transaction.**

![Borrow MetaMask transaction request](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-09-borrow-metamask.png)

**4. Wait for the portal to refresh the position preview and health factor.**

![Borrow confirmed](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-10-borrow-processing.png)

The borrowed asset arrives in your Ethereum wallet. The app now shows your **health factor**, a single number summarizing how close the position is to liquidation. A higher number is safer.

## Step 5: Repay the debt (Aave v4 application layer)

To close the position, repay what you borrowed.

From the **Loans** section, click **Repay**:

* **Repay full** closes the entire borrow and unlocks redemption.
* **Repay partial** pays down a portion; the position stays open with reduced debt.

![Repay form](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-13-repay-form.png)

:::info Two MetaMask prompts
Repaying usually shows an ERC-20 spending cap approval first, then the repayment transaction. Approve a small buffer because interest can accrue while the transaction is pending; any excess stays in your wallet.
:::

![Repay spending cap approval](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-14-repay-approve.png)

![Repay confirmed](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-15-repay-confirm.png)

Interest accrues continuously until repayment, so the exact amount due is slightly higher than the principal borrowed. After full repayment, the vault is eligible for redemption.

## Step 6: Withdraw and redeem the BTC (peg-out — TBV protocol layer)

Redeeming releases the underlying BTC from the vault back to your Bitcoin wallet. This step crosses back from the Aave v4 application layer into the TBV protocol layer.

From the **Collateral** section, select an **in use** vault and click **Withdraw**.

![Withdraw form — selected vault](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-16-withdraw-form.png)

Review the withdrawal amount, network fee rate, VP commission, and the nominated Bitcoin address.

![Withdraw review](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-17-withdraw-review.png)

Confirm the Ethereum transaction.

![Withdraw MetaMask transaction request](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-18-withdraw-confirm.png)

The redemption then runs in three protocol-side phases:

1. **Claim.** The Vault Provider generates a zero-knowledge proof in the background showing the debt is repaid, then submits a redemption claim on Bitcoin. The app shows progress.
2. **Challenge period.** A window of about **3 days** opens during which a Vault Keeper or Universal Challenger may dispute the claim. If no valid challenge appears, the claim finalizes.
3. **Payout.** Your BTC arrives at the address recorded in the vault.

![Withdraw initiated](/img/trustless-bitcoin-vault/use-for-lending/quickstart-19-redemption-progress.png)

The 3-day window is inherent to the proof-verification mechanism.

## If something stalls

Common scenarios and where to look:

* **Pre-PegIn still pending after a few hours:** check the Bitcoin block explorer (see [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx)). If the transaction is propagated but not confirmed, wait — signet block times can be irregular.
* **Vault stuck in Pending past ~24 hours:** the off-chain setup likely failed. The vault will transition to Expired and your peg-in fee will be refunded. See the refund path on [Create a vault](./create-a-vault.mdx) for BTC recovery.
* **Wallet won't connect or transaction rejected:** verify the wallet is on the correct network (see [Setup](../testnet-info/setup.mdx) for chain IDs).
* **Vault Provider not responding during redemption:** the depositor self-claim path applies. See [Withdraw & redeem](./withdraw-and-redeem.mdx).

For anything else, ask in the public-testnet support channel listed on [Community & support](../reference/community-and-support.mdx).

## You've completed a full cycle

Wallets connected, signet BTC locked, internal accounting supplied, stablecoins borrowed, repaid, and BTC recovered.
