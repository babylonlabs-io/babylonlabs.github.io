# Fees

:::info Draft — under review
Fee values are sourced from the canonical Beta Testnet Smart Contract Parameters reference. Borrowing interest rates come from the Aave v4 rate model and depend on utilization; a TBV-level protocol fee is taken on top of accrued interest.
:::

This page describes the fees a depositor encounters across the Trustless Bitcoin Vault (TBV) lifecycle: vault creation, redemption, borrowing interest, and liquidation. The accounting collateral token throughout is vaultBTC. For deployed contract addresses, see [Contract addresses](../testnet-info/contract-addresses). For the live numeric values of parameters that determine these fees, see [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters).

## Summary

| Fee | When charged | Paid by | Current value (Testnet) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Peg-in fee (4 components) | Vault creation | Depositor | ~0.00486 ETH total, paid to Vault Provider + Universal Challengers + Application Vault Keepers + Protocol |
| Vault Provider commission | Vault redemption (peg-out) | Depositor (deducted from payout BTC) | Set per-VP, ≥ 1% (current minimum floor; under review) |
| Borrow interest | Continuously while debt is outstanding | Borrower | Set by Aave v4 rate model (see [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters)) |
| Protocol interest fee | Continuously, deducted from accrued interest | Borrower (effectively) | 10% of accrued Aave interest |
| Liquidation bonus | Liquidation | Position being liquidated | Up to 10% (max `maxLiquidationBonus`) |
| Liquidation redemption fee | Direct-redemption liquidation path | Position being liquidated | 5% (`liquidationRedemptionFeeBps`) |
| Liquidation fee on vaultBTC collateral | Liquidation | — | `0`. Structurally not applicable |
| LLP commission (BTCVaultSwap) | Liquidation via LLP (BTCVaultSwap) | Position being liquidated | 30% of arbitrageur margin (`discountCommissionBps`) |
| LLP sell discount (BTCVaultSwap) | Liquidation via LLP (BTCVaultSwap) | LLP (eats into depositor's fairness payment) | 1.5% (`sellDiscountBps`) |

## Vault lifecycle fees

### Peg-in fee

Charged once on vault creation. The peg-in fee is not a single payment to the protocol — it is split into four components, each paid to a different party that participates in the peg-in setup. The total is collected at peg-in submission and distributed by the `FeeEscrow` contract.

| Component | Recipient | Testnet value (wei) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `vpFeeRate` | Vault Provider for this vault | `1_285_714_285_714_286` |
| `ucFeeRate` | Universal Challengers (split among the registered set) | `1_285_714_285_714_286` |
| `avkFeeRate` | Application Vault Keepers (split among the application's registered set) | `1_285_714_285_714_286` |
| `protocolPegInFeeRate` | Protocol | `1_000_000_000_000_000` |
| **Total per peg-in** | — | **~4.86 × 10^15 wei (~0.00486 ETH)** |

* **What it covers:** protocol cost of setting up the pre-signed transaction graph, off-chain coordination work by participants, and on-chain Bitcoin operations involved in activation. Compensating each participant directly is what makes the participant set economically motivated to complete peg-in.
* **When charged:** on successful peg-in submission.
* **Refundable:** yes, if the vault fails to reach `Verified` status because off-chain setup is incomplete by `pegInAckTimeout`. The vault transitions to `Expired` and the peg-in fee is returned. Non-refundable in the normal path: once the vault is Verified or Active, the fee is final, including if the depositor later redeems early or is liquidated.

Separately, Vault Providers pay a one-time on-chain **registration fee** when they first register (`vpRegistrationFee`, ~$50 worth of ETH). This is paid by the VP, not by depositors per peg-in.

### Vault Provider commission (peg-out)

Charged on vault redemption, deducted from the BTC paid out to the recipient. The commission is set per Vault Provider — depositors who select a VP at peg-in implicitly agree to that VP's commission rate.

* **What it covers:** ZK proof generation, on-chain Bitcoin operations involved in peg-out, and the operator workload required to claim and settle the redemption. The Vault Provider receives the commission as compensation for the proof and the multi-day coordination.
* **When charged:** at peg-out settlement, deducted from the final Payout transaction.
* **How it is set:** each Vault Provider sets its own commission, at or above the protocol's minimum floor `minVpCommissionBps`. Different VPs may compete on commission rate. The commission for a vault's VP is locked at vault creation — the same VP serves the vault for its lifetime.
* **Current floor:** 1% (`minVpCommissionBps` = 100 BPS). The minimum is under review and may be removed entirely, in which case VPs would price commissions freely.

## Borrowing

### Borrow interest (Aave v4 rate model)

Charged continuously on outstanding debt. The rate is determined by the Aave v4 borrowing rate model and depends on utilization of the borrowed asset's reserve.

* **What determines the rate:** Aave v4 reserve utilization, base rate, and slope parameters configured at the Aave v4 Hub for each borrowable asset (USDC, USDT, WBTC). See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters) for the per-asset values.
* **How it accrues:** per block, on the principal owed.
* **Where to check the current rate:** the demo portal surfaces the live rate at the time of borrowing; on-chain, query the reserve's interest rate strategy.
* **How interest works in TBV:** see [Borrow & repay](../use-for-lending/borrow-and-repay) and [Aave v4 integration](../technical-details/aave-v4-integration).

### Protocol interest fee

The protocol charges a percentage of the accrued Aave interest as a protocol fee (`InterestFeeBps`). This is layered on top of the Aave rate.

* **What it covers:** protocol operating costs and revenue for ongoing operations.
* **How it is collected:** on each repayment, the protocol-fee portion of accrued interest is collected first, then principal reduction applies. Partial repayments may not reduce principal at all until the protocol's interest share is paid.
* **Current value:** 10% (`InterestFeeBps` = 1000 BPS) on Testnet.
* **Effective cost to the borrower:** the Aave headline rate is what utilization computes. The protocol takes 10% of each interest accrual as it accumulates, so total interest paid by the borrower equals the Aave rate (the protocol fee comes out of the interest accrued, not added on top).

## Liquidation

### Liquidation bonus

A discount applied to seized collateral when a position is liquidated. The bonus is the incentive for liquidators to act on liquidatable positions. It scales dynamically as the position deteriorates further past the liquidation threshold and is capped at `maxLiquidationBonus`.

* **Paid by:** the position being liquidated (the bonus is the gap between debt repaid and collateral seized at market value).
* **Scaling:** larger as the gap between health factor and target health factor grows.
* **Cap:** `maxLiquidationBonus` = 10% on Testnet. See [Protocol parameters](../testnet-info/protocol-parameters).

### Liquidation redemption fee

An additional protocol fee on the direct-redemption liquidation path (when the liquidator is a registered arbitrageur with a Bitcoin redeem key and redeems the seized vault directly, bypassing the LLP).

* **Paid by:** the position being liquidated, deducted from the value returned to the depositor.
* **Current value:** 5% (`liquidationRedemptionFeeBps` = 500 BPS).
* **When it applies:** only on the `liquidate(...)` path with a direct BTC redeem key. On the permissionless `liquidateWithLLP(...)` path, the LLP commission applies instead.

### Liquidation fee on vaultBTC collateral

Set to `0` by design. Each vaultBTC unit corresponds to a Bitcoin UTXO that cannot be partially extracted, so an Aave-style proportional liquidation fee cannot be applied to vaultBTC collateral. The economic cost of being liquidated is captured by the liquidation bonus and, depending on the path, the LLP commission or the liquidation redemption fee.

### LLP commission (BTCVaultSwap)

When seized vaults are routed through a Liquidation Liquidity Provider (LLP), such as the default BTCVaultSwap, the LLP charges a commission for taking the vault and supplying the borrowed asset back to the protocol.

* **What it covers:** the LLP's spread for warehousing the seized vault and providing instant liquidity to liquidators.
* **Paid by:** the position being liquidated, deducted from the value returned to the depositor in the fairness payment step.
* **Current value:** 30% of the arbitrageur's profit margin (`discountCommissionBps` = 3000 BPS).

### LLP sell discount

The discount the LLP applies when it pays liquidators in WBTC immediately. The LLP supplies discounted WBTC liquidity (relative to oracle BTC value) to liquidators, then aims to acquire the seized vault later at a price that recoups this discount plus its commission.

* **What it covers:** LLP risk of holding a seized BTC vault while waiting for an arbitrageur to acquire it.
* **Effect on the depositor:** reduces the value available for fairness payment.
* **Current value:** 1.5% (`sellDiscountBps` = 150 BPS).

Liquidations on TBV use a partial-position model when a position holds multiple vaults: only the subset of vaults needed to restore the health factor is seized. The rest remain with the depositor. See [Liquidation risk](../use-for-lending/liquidation-risk) for what this means in practice and how fairness payments return any over-seizure to the depositor.

## Ethereum gas

Every Ethereum-side action (peg-in request, activation, add collateral, borrow, repay, withdraw, redeem) incurs Ethereum gas fees paid to validators. These are independent of TBV protocol fees and depend on network conditions. On Testnet, gas is paid in testnet ETH from the faucet.
